Twin Lakes Dam releases water seasonally from the upper basin (transmountain diversion), creating the larger annual flows that make the Arkansas runnable. Most gauges are seasonal — meaningful only during the release window (roughly April–August). Flow is largely release-dependent, not purely snowmelt.

Below Granite (USGS 07087050) is the first significant gauge for the upper runs. Pine Creek (Class V) is the first major feature below Granite — this is a distinct Class V section/reach, not just a rapid within The Numbers.

**How to apply:** When building the Arkansas corridor seeder, treat Pine Creek as its own reach entry (Class V, below Granite), upstream of The Numbers. The Granite gauge controls both. Note the seasonal/release-dependent nature in all reach Notes.
